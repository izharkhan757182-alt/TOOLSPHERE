Frequently asked questions

What do the BMI categories mean?

Under 18.5 is underweight, 18.5–24.9 is normal, 25–29.9 is overweight, and 30+ is considered obese, per WHO guidelines.

Is BMI always accurate?

BMI is a general screening tool and doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or body composition, so it's not a full picture of individual health.
