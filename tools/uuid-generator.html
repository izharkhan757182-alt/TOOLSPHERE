Frequently asked questions

What is a UUID?

A Universally Unique Identifier is a 128-bit value, typically shown as 32 hex digits, used to uniquely identify records without needing a central authority.

Are these UUIDs guaranteed to be unique?

UUID v4 values are randomly generated with enough entropy that collisions are astronomically unlikely in practice.
